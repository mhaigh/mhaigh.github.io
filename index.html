About

Summary

I am a Mathematics PhD student at Imperial College London, specialising in large-scale oceanography. I am particulary interested in the developing an understanding of interaction between large and small scales in the shallow-water model.

Education history

2015 - present: Mathematics PhD student, Imperial College London. Supervisor: Dr Pavel Berloff

2011 - 2015: MMath Integrated Masters in Mathematics, University of Leeds.

Research

Shallow-water double-gyre regime

A coherent meandering eastward jet is relatively simple to simulate using the quasi-geostrophic model, but surprisingly difficult using the shallow-water model. Part of my research involves attempting to attain a realistic double-gyre regime in the shallow-water model, and understanding the role of small-scale and large-scale fluxes in the system. For this branch of my research, I use the MOM6 primitive equation model. Left: upper layer small-scale potential vorticity in a high-resolution double-gyre simulation.

Idealised shallow-water dynamics

I am also interested in the nature of Rossby waves in an idealised single-layer shallow-water model. Specifically, I aim to develop an understanding of nonlinear self-interactions of Rossby waves, and how this depends on the large-scale background flow. Right: interpretation of the Rossby wave field via 'eddy shape' parameters. The left-hand panel quantifies the eddy zonal elongation, the right-hand panel quanties the eddy tilt.
